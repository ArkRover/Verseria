Player personality

Some dialogue decision shifts your alignment between virtue and sin, altering quests, alliances, and how the world judges you.

Charity (Virtue) vs Greed (Sin): Giving gold to the desperate may earn you a hidden ally; hoarding wealth could mark you as a target for thieves. Chastity (Virtue) vs Lust (Sin): Rejecting seduction preserves honor, but indulging might unlock forbidden favors—at the cost of reputation. Temperance (Virtue) vs Gluttony (Sin): Sharing food with allies strengthens bonds; devouring resources alone leaves others starving. Diligence (Virtue) vs Sloth (Sin): Laboring to rebuild a village rewards you with loyalty; ignoring pleas erodes trust. Patience (Virtue) vs Wrath (Sin): Sparing a foe's life may lead to redemption; executing them ensures vengeance—but breeds new enemies. Kindness (Virtue) vs Envy (Sin): Celebrating another's success gains their aid; sabotaging them out of jealousy isolates you. Humility (Virtue) vs Pride (Sin): Admitting fault earns respect; arrogant boasts provoke challenges or betrayal.
